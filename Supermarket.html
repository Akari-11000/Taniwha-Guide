Supermarket & Shopping Mall

「 Must-Check Mall & Supermarkets in Christchurch, New Zealand 」

Supermarket

Pak N Save

Pak N Save is a New Zealand discount food supermarket warehouse chain owned by the Foodstuffs cooperative. It is one of the cheapest supermarket in Christchurch.

It is one of the three main supermarket chains in New Zealand, alongside Woolworths New Zealand and New World (the latter is also owned by Foodstuffs). There is a total of 58 Pak'nSave stores throughout the country.

Countdown

Countdown was an Australian-owned New Zealand full-service supermarket chain and subsidiary of Woolworths New Zealand. It is one of two supermarket chains in New Zealand.Countdown Supermarkets tend to be expensive however they have a good international section and offer a lot of options for vegans.

Now the Countdown has rebranded in Woolworths and they offer online shopping.

FreshChoice

FreshChoice Barrington is bright, colourful and modern, with a full offering of departments including in-store bakery, delicatessen, produce, butchery and an extensive range of wine and beer.

Customers enjoy the store, which is light and spacious but with a warm, marketplace feel that is often absent in the ‘big barn’ formats.

FreshChoice supermarkets are all locally owned and operated as well as served by local people. We are proud of being local and value being part of the local community with stores. With 50 stores across New Zealand.

Shopping Mall

The Tannery

Located in the beautifully restored Woolston Tanneries, this quirky retail centre is a hub of all things Kiwi-made and vintage.

Here, you can get your hands on decorative antiques, unique art and epic designer clothing! After you’re all shopped out, treat yourself to a spa treatment, a bite to eat or catch a movie – all located within The Tannery.

The Colombo

Edgy with a contemporary feel, The Colombo is home to an art house cinema and boutique shopping.

Eating at their European inspired food court is a dream! Especially if you’re relaxing at the large communal tables – a good break from cruising around an awesome selection of stores.
