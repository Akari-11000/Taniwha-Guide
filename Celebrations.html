「 Seasonal Celebrations in Christchurch, New Zealand 」

Kite Day

International Kite Day begins every year on January 14 during Makar Sankranti and ends on January 15. Gujarat has declared the dates a public holiday so that people can join in the festivities. Kite makers start preparing for the festival months in advance. Markets are packed with kite aficionados and festival goers buying supplies days before the festivities.

When : 14th January

MATARIKI: MĀORI NEW YEAR

Matariki is known as the Māori New Year in Te Ao Māori (the Māori world view).

Closely connected with the maramataka (the Māori lunar calendar), the reappearance of the Matariki stars in the early morning sky brings the past year to a close and marks the beginning of the new year.

When : 13th - 28th June

KidFest

KidFest is about the power of books in each child’s life. Our day-long, activity-filled event for kids of all ages, and their parents, features authors and illustrators who share their creative passion with our most important readers! the Festival has brought the community together to celebrate the power of the written word.

KidsFest is proudly produced by Christchurch City Council, with most events and activities run independently by a variety of organisations, businesses and groups in the Christchurch, Selwyn and Waimakariri Districts.

When : 6th - 21st July

Easter Monday

In New Zealand, Easter Monday is the last day of a four-day weekend, which includes Good Friday. Many people return from short trips out of town, while others relax at home. Many families take part in an Easter egg hunt and other Easter related activities. Chocolate eggs are especially popular to eat. Some churches hold special Easter Monday services.

When : 21st April

Christmas

Christmas is celebrated on December 25 and is both a sacred religious holiday and a worldwide cultural and commercial phenomenon. For two millennia, people around the world have been observing it with traditions and practices that are both religious and secular in nature. Christians celebrate Christmas Day as the anniversary of the birth of Jesus of Nazareth, a spiritual leader whose teachings form the basis of their religion. Popular customs include exchanging gifts, decorating Christmas trees, attending church, sharing meals with family and friends and, of course, waiting for Santa Claus to arrive. December 25—Christmas Day—has been a federal holiday in the United States since 1870.

When : 25th November
